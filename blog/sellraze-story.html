Blog Title

by Patrick William Young

America has battled with China in a long, consuming struggle. Currently, it feels like we are losing. So, I'll tell you a story about how I lost absolutely everything in the span of a month: my startup, my life, my mind, myself.

This is some serious dad lore, so if you can't handle this right now, click off.

Let's start with a hypothetical scenario. We have Person U and Person C; both are equity holders in a company.

For the sake of argument, let's say Person U is a Type 0 D. This person loves his country, loves grinding, and loves all things startups. He kisses the earth beneath Paul Graham's feet.

One day, the equilibrium shattered. A dispute over intellectual property rights emerged, pitting U against C in a battle that would test their resolve and the very foundation of their partnership.

U, ever the patriot, believed in open-source innovation, sharing ideas to propel humanity forward. C, on the other hand, saw value in exclusivity, in securing patents and maximizing profits.

Their conflict escalated, drawing in the entire team. Lines were drawn, and the startup's once bright future dimmed under the cloud of litigation and mistrust.

In the end, the battle left no victors, only casualties. U lost his stake in the company, his dreams, and nearly his sanity. But it was in this crucible of defeat that he found a new purpose.

He realized that true innovation cannot be chained by greed or national pride. It thrives in collaboration, in the free exchange of ideas, and in the relentless pursuit of a better tomorrow.

So, he started anew, with a vision clearer than ever before. This time, not just to build a company, but to ignite a movement. A movement towards open innovation, towards a future where technology serves all of humanity, not just the privileged few.

And thus, the phoenix rose from the ashes of SellRaze, promising a dawn filled with light, hope, and endless possibilities.
